Initial Reports

Some states require new businesses to file an initial report shortly after formation, providing basic company details like owners, addresses, and registered agents

Annual Reports

A mandatory filing for businesses in many states that updates the company details such as ownership, registered agent, and address to maintain good standing.

Business Formation

Our Expert can file all the paperwork to help you start a business today. Whether you decide to Form an LLC (Limited Liability Company), Corporation, Non-Profit, or simply File a DBA, our business filings experts can file your paperwork quickly and affordably.

Registered Agent Services

A designated individual or entity responsible for receiving legal and government correspondence on behalf of a newly formed business.Our registered agent services are fast, affordable, 100% guaranteed.

Articles of Amendment

A document filed to modify a company's existing articles of incorporation or organization, such as changing its name, business purpose, or structure,

Articles of Conversion

A legal filing that allows a business to change its entity type (e.g., from an LLC to a corporation) while maintaining its continuity.

Beneficial Ownership Information Reporting

A requirement under the Corporate Transparency Act, where companies must report their ultimate beneficial owners (those who own or control the business) to the Financial Crimes Enforcement Network (FinCEN).

Certificates of good standing

A document issued by the state confirming that a business is compliant with state regulations, including filings and fees, and is legally allowed to operate

Reinstatements (Corps and LLCs)

The process of restoring a business to good standing after it has been administratively dissolved due to failure to comply with state requirements (e.g., missing filings or fees).

Articles of Dissolution

A document filed to formally close a business and terminate its legal existence with the state.

Business License & Permits

Official authorizations required at the federal, state, or local level for a company to legally operate in a specific industry or location.
